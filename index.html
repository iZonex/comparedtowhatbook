Check it yourself

Six and four

Your wallet identifies an address by its ends — the first characters and the last. Here are two, exactly as it would show them to you.

A transfer you just made0x7A3F…4E210x7A3F9d2E5b1c8f0A6D4b3E7c9A1f0B2c5D8e4E21
Another, from the same history0x7A3F…4E210x7A3F0c1B7a4D9e2F8c3A5b6E1d0F4a9C2b7E4E21

Same address, or different?

The ends are identical — 0x7A3F … 4E21 — and that is all your wallet ever showed you. The middles are completely different. An attacker slips a lookalike into your history so you copy the wrong one and send your money to them. It is the shortest chapter in the book, because the whole mechanism is one sentence.
